§ 04 · FAQ The ones we actually get

What's a token? How much is a million?

A token is roughly 4 characters of Latin text or ~2 of Arabic text. A million tokens is around 750 pages of A4 reading text. Most chatbots use 5–20k tokens per conversation; a RAG agent answering one zakat question uses ~3–8k tokens end-to-end (embed + retrieve + generate + verify).

Why halala-grained billing?

The smallest currency unit lets us settle accurately at the request level — no rounding up, no minimum charge per call. A 240-token answer should cost what a 240-token answer costs, even if it's less than a riyal.

Can I pay in USDC?

Yes — USDC on Base L2 only, deposited to your tenant's wallet address. Conversion is at the moving peg (currently 1 USDC = SAR 3.751, ±0.1% spread). SAR is drawn first; USDC only when the SAR balance reaches zero.

What happens to unused subscription tokens?

They expire at the end of each billing month. Overage rolls to PAYG rates automatically — no surprises, no service interruption. If you consistently under-use a tier, downgrade in console at any time; if you consistently over-use, the calculator above will suggest the next step.

Are there per-request minimums or hidden fees?

No. No per-request minimum, no egress charge, no PrivateLink markup, no monthly platform fee on top of PAYG. The PAYG bill is exactly tokens × rate. Subscription bills are exactly tier × month + overage.

What about the GPU rental I saw in v1?

DCP v2 is inference-first — the GPU mesh is the substrate, not the product. Renting raw GPUs is now a supply-side offering on the Providers page. If you have an existing GPU-hour contract, it's honoured through its term; new customers buy tokens.
